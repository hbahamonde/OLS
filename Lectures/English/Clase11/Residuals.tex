...let's see how the OLS model, but in matrix form. For simplicity, we will just focus on the first three countries.



\begin{equation*}
\begin{bmatrix}
-1.6370\\
-1.3420\\
0.5433\\
\vdots\\
i_{N}
\end{bmatrix}_{y_{i}} \ =\ -0.58566_{\beta 0} - 0.19107_{\beta_{1}}\begin{bmatrix}
0.6156\\
0.9518\\
-4.9760\\
\vdots\\
i_{N}
\end{bmatrix}_{x_{i}} \ +\ \begin{bmatrix}
-0.9337127\\
-0.5744742\\
0.1781864\\
\vdots\\
i_{N}
\end{bmatrix}_{\epsilon_{i}}
\end{equation*}

Since $\hat y_{i} = \beta_{0}+\beta_{1}\text{Democracy}_{i}-\epsilon_{i}$, then it's easy to see that $\epsilon_{i}= y_{i}-\hat y_{i}$. Or in simple, the error ($\epsilon_{i}$) is the difference between the observed $y_{i}$ and the predicted $\hat y_{i}$. It makes complete sense: it quantifies how ``wrong'' my model is.
